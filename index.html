Unveiling Political Polarization in the U.S. Congress (1879–2023): A Time-Series Analysis

Abstarct

Understanding political polarization is crucial for strengthening democratic processes and legislative effectiveness. This study analyzes long-term, cyclical, and periodic trends in U.S. congressional polarization from 1879 to 2023, offering insights into its historical patterns and future trajectory. Using DW-NOMINATE scores from Voteview dataset, we examine ideological shifts across key moments in history. Long-term trends reveal a steady trends in polarization, while cyclical patterns emerge during economic crises, and periodic shifts align with election cycles. Our analysis combines time-series methods and clustering techniques to identify persistent trends, periods of ideological convergence, and potential future outcomes. By providing a quantitative perspective on how polarization evolves over time, this study highlights its broader impact on democratic institutions and offers a framework for further research into its underlying causes and consequences.
